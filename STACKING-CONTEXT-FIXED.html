üí° Why This Happened:

CSS has a concept called "stacking context" - when an element has position + z-index, it creates a 3D layer. Everything inside that layer is isolated from elements outside it.

Your header created layer 10, and your menu (even with z-index 2 billion!) was stuck inside layer 10. Other page elements rendered in the default layer (0), which appeared AFTER layer 10 in rendering order, covering your menu!
